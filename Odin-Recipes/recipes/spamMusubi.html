Spam Musubi

Description

This is an easy recipe to make with minimal ingredients, simple steps, and no special equipment needed. It's ready in 30 minutes from start to finish. It makes very good meal prep, it's handheld and portable, and it has nice, balanced flavors; it's sweet, salty, chewy, and filling. A perfect combo!

Ingredients

    Main
  • 2 cups cooked rice (1:1 rice to water ratio)
  • 1 can Spam
    • Sauce
  • 3 tbsp Soy Sauce
  • 3 tbsp Mirin OR 3 tbsp rice vinegar mixed with 1 1/2
  • 3 tbsp Sugar
    • Other
  • 3 eggs
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Furikake
  • 2 nori sheets, cut into 8 equal strips.

Steps

  1. Make the sauce by mixing the soy sauce, mirin, and sugar together in a small bowl until combined. Set this aside.
  2. Slice the SPAM into 8 even rectangular pieces lengthwise.
  3. Cook the SPAM in a pan over medium heat until golden and crispy on both sides (roughly 2-3 min per side).
  4. Lower the heat and pour the sauce into the same pan we're cooking the SPAM in. Simmer until it thickens into a glossy glaze and remove from heat.
  5. Make the egg layer by whisking the eggs with salt and pepper and pouring it into a lightly oiled pan over medium-low heat, cooking it into a thin, even sheet. Use the empty SPAM cn to cut out 8 rectangular egg pieces.
  6. Assemble your masterpieces by lining the inside of the SPAM can with plastic wrap. Add a couple of teaspoons of rice and compress it firmly. Sprinkle with furikake and add one egg piece and one SPAM piece. Lift everything out using the plastic wrap and then wrap each musubi tightly with a strip of nori.
Storage & Reheating

Wrapped individually in plastic wrap and stored in the fridge, these can stay for up to two days. The nori will soften over time, which is perfectly normal and still delicious, however you can also wrap your musubi when ready to eat.
To reheat, there are two good methods.

  • Microwave (best method): Reheat wrapped or loosely covered for 20 to 30 seconds, until warmed through.
  • Pan: Unwrap and heat in a dry pan over medium heat for 1 to 2 minutes per side to crisp up the outside again.
