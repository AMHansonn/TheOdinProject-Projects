No-Knead Korean Salt Bread (Shio Pan)

Description

Soft, fluffy rolls with a buttery, crisp bottom and a molten butter center, made entirely without a stand mixer! This is a perfect recipe for beginners who want to learn how to make bread and dough without the heavy, expensive equipment. It creates that same signature flavor and texture as traditional salt bread, creating a crispy and buttery bottom with a soft inside. These also freeze very well as a bonus!

Ingredients

  • 400g bread flour
  • 120g cake flour
  • 365g cold water
  • 26g sugar
  • 10g salt
  • 30g non-fat milk powder
  • 40g cold unsalted butter
  • 7g instant dry yeast
  • 12 x 15g butter chunks (roughly a stick and a half of butter)
  • Pretzel salt

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, combine the bread flour, cake flour, salt, milk powder, and yeast. Add cool tap water and mix until no dry flour remains. The dough will be shaggy and sticky--this is normal.
  2. Cover and rest for 25 minutes to allow gluten to begin forming on its' own
  3. Add the softened butter in 2 to 3 additions, gently kneading by hand until fully incorporated and the dough comes together into one cohesive mass. It doesn't have to be smooth just yet.
  4. Cover and rest for another 25 minutes
  5. Perform 3 sets of stretch and folds, spaced 25 minutes apart. Fold the top of the dough to the center, then the bottom to the center, then left to right. Flip seam side down, cover, and rest 25 minutes.
  6. The dough will become noticably smoother and more elastic with each fold. After the third set you want the surface to look smooth and the dough to pass the windowpane test. If it's not quite there after the third fold, do a few extra coil folds or slap and folds.
  7. After the final fold, let the dough rest until it increases to roughly 1.5 times its' original volume. It should feel lighter, smoother, and slightly jiggly when you shake the bowl.
  8. Degas the dough and divide into 80 gram portions. Roll each into a smooth ball, cover, and let the rolls bench rest for 15 minutes starting from the first ball you shaped
  9. Roll each ball into a long teardrop shape and then flatten the narrow bottom half with a rolling pin while gently pulling downward. Shape should be roughly triangular.
  10. Place a butter chunk at the wide end and roll the dough down toward the point with gentle tension, placing the rolls seam side down on a non-stick baking tray with no parchment paper. Leave space between each roll.
  11. Cover and let rise until doubled and puffy.
  12. Preheat oven to 450 degrees F. Spray rolls lightly with water and sprinkle pretzel salt on top. When the oven is preheated, spray each roll generously with about 20 pumps of water. Lower the oven to 400 degrees F and bake for 12 to 15 minutes.
  13. Eat immediately for the crispiest bottoms.
Storage & Reheating

Skip the fridge as it dries out the dough.
If freezing the dough, let the rolls cool completely, place in an airtight bag, and freeze for up to 2 months.
If reheating from room temp, air fry at 350 for 2 to 4 minutes. If reheating from frozen, air fry at 350 degrees F for 5 to 8 minutes, flipping halfway.
